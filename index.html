What is a DAO?

A DAO, or “Decentralized Autonomous Organization,” is a community-led entity with no central authority. It is fully autonomous and transparent: smart contracts lay the foundational rules, execute the agreed upon decisions, and at any point, proposals, voting, and even the very code itself can be publicly audited.
The rules of the DAO are established by a core team of community members through the use of smart contracts. These smart contracts lay out the foundational framework by which the DAO is to operate. They are highly visible, verifiable, and publicly auditable so any potential member can fully understand how the protocol is to function at every step.

History

On 30 October 1939 it was founded as Lérida Balompié-AEM.
It became Unión Deportiva Lérida in 1947, after a merger with CD Leridano.
1950-51 was the first topflight experience for the club at Primera División of Spain.
1993–94 was the second topflight experience for the club at Primera División of Spain; It won 1–0 over the
FC Barcelona Dream Team at the Camp Nou, and won against Real Madrid, 2–1 at home.
It holds home matches at Camp d´Esports, with a 13,500-seat capacity. Since 2011, the club was reformed as Lleida Esportiu.
In 2022 makes history becoming the first professional football club to be managed completely by a DAO.
